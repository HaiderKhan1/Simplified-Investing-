Market Prospect Ratios


Market Prospect ratios are mainly used by investors to compare publicly traded companies’ stock prices with other financial measures such as earnings per share and dividend payouts. Not only that, market prospect ratios help investors analyze current stock price trends, and dividend measurements which they can use to determine future market value of shares.


Earnings Per Share (EPS)


Measures the amount of money each share of stock would receive if all of the profits were distributed to the outstanding shares at the end of the year. It also shows how profitable a company is on a shareholder basis. After calculating a higher EPS ratio is better than a lower one as the company is more profitable.

Price Earnings P/E Ratio


Shows what the market is willing to pay for a stock based on its current earnings. Investors use the P/E ratio to evaluate a stock’s fair market value by predicting future earnings per share. Furthermore, it also allows investors to analyze how much they should pay for a stock based on its current earnings. A company with a high P/E ratio indicates positive future performance and growth, and a low P/E ratio indicated poor performance and future growth.

Dividend Payout Ratio


Measures the percentage of net income that is distributed to shareholders in the form of dividends during the year. This ratio is imperative for investors as it shows if the company pays out a reasonable portion of net income to investors. Furthermore, when analyzing a high payout ratio is good; however, a consistent trend in this ratio is more important. Therefore, calculate this ratio for multiple years and analyze whether this ratio remains consistent or whether it’s declining.

Dividend Payout Ratio


The dividend yield is a financial ratio that measures the amount of cash dividends distributed to common shareholders relative to the market value per share. Moreover, investors can use the dividend yield formula to analyze their return on investments in stocks, and know how much dividends they are getting for every dollar that the stock is worth. When analyzing, a high dividend yield pays it’s investors a large dividend compared to the fair market value. It is also important to understand that this ratio varies in different industries i.e tech industries rarely pay dividends.
